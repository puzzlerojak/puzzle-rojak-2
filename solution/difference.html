Solution: Difference in Opinion
Answer: RAILROAD

Written by Jonathan

Several of the descriptions are pretty obvious what they are, or at least the movie franchise, e.g. the Chitauri Leviathan might be a good place to start. We quickly realize that there are 2 lines referring to the same movie, one of the more obvious ones of this fact are the 2 James Bond references to Casino Royale, since playing cards and Vesper both point to the same movie. We also realize that every line has either all the Ss or all the Ws missing from the start of each words, we keep that information to use later, but note that as per the flavortext, the number of times they are missing do not matter. We identify the movies as follows, and index into the movie name by the indices provided.

Casino Royale 3 S 61

We get the cluephrase YOUTUBE CINEMA INS. Googling some will immediately point you to Cinemasins, where we find the sin number associated with the sins mentioned in the descriptions given. We find that only half of them, specifically the lines with the Ss missing, can be found. Either having already realized it, searching more, or by applying the W in the same way to the cluephrase, we can then find the other youtube channel, Cinemawins, where we find the sin number associated with the wins mentioned in the other half of the descriptions. Lastly, we can then take the sin/win difference, as hinted by the puzzle title (the first instance of sin/win is always higher than the second instance).

Movie First score Second score Difference A1Z26
Mission: Impossible 28 10 18 R
How to train your dragon 23 22 1 A
Ready Player One 35 26 9 I
Spider-Man Homecoming 14 2 12 L
Baby Driver 56 38 18 R
Inception 32 17 15 O
Kung Fu Panda 26 25 1 A
Casino Royale 65 61 4 D

The first half of the descriptions are alphabetical by movie name, and so we order by the second half, giving the answer RAILROAD.

Author's Notes

I thought the aha of getting cinemas sins/wins was cute, but as a solver who strongly dislikes grindy puzzles I tried to make it as easy as possible to extract. The extraction ordering and method is pretty clear, hopefully teams that do solve this puzzle can do so with about half the movies. In addition, because the exact line is used in the YouTube video, searching the youtube transcript would save a lot of time from watching the videos themselves. This was another puzzle idea that I wanted to write for Puzzle Rojak 1 but couldn't because of the long puzzle answers then. I have watched many CinemaSins videos, and feel that the CinemaWins channel is kind of underrated.

The extraction for this puzzle was inspired by this Galactic puzzle, which has to be one of my personal favorites.
